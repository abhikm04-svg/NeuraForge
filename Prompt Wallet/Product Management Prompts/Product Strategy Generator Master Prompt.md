# Strategy Document Template 
## Context 
List the 5–6 key pieces of context that shape this strategy (market shifts, competitive threats, customer trends, internal goals, prior learnings). Overwhelm with context so no reader misinterprets the situation. 
## Problem / Opportunity 
Summarize in 1–2 sentences the problem (or opportunity) that this strategy is meant to address. Highlight both the customer pain/gain and the business imperative. 
## Vision 
A concise statement of the future state we want to create, ideally inspiring and directional. This is why the strategy matters, not the execution details. 
## Strategic Pillars 
List 3–5 core pillars or themes that define how we will pursue the vision. Each pillar should describe what we’ll prioritize, why it matters, and how it differentiates us. 
## Market and Competitive Landscape 
Analyze relevant market size, growth trends, and competitor moves. Call out where we have advantage vs. where we’re vulnerable. Use simple models or data-backed estimates where possible. 
## Customer Insights 
Summarize what we know about user needs, behaviors, and segments. Include evidence (quant + qual). Show how these insights directly tie to the strategy. 
## Goals and Success Metrics 
Describe measurable goals this strategy aims to achieve (e.g., growth, retention, NPS, revenue, engagement). Clarify the north star metric, secondary metrics, and guardrails. 
## Tradeoffs and Non-goals 
List explicit areas this strategy will not address, and explain why. Highlight the tradeoffs we are consciously making. 
## Strategic Bets and Roadmap 
Outline the major initiatives / bets to bring this strategy to life. Place them on a rough timeline (near-term, mid-term, long-term). Clarify sequencing and interdependencies. 
## Risks and Contingencies 
Highlight key risks (market, execution, adoption, org) that could derail the strategy, and potential mitigations. 
## Alignment and Next Steps 
Explain how this strategy aligns with company mission, leadership priorities, and product portfolio. Suggest immediate next steps to validate, refine, and socialize the strategy.
